Essential Orienteering Equipment

Explore the mandatory equipment needed for navigation, tracking, apparel, and safety.

Navigation Equipment
Orienteering Map
A highly detailed, large-scale topographic map showing terrain contours, vegetation density, and water features, alongside the specific course checkpoints.
Orienteering Compass
A specialized compass—usually a transparent baseplate or a thumb compass—used to align the map with magnetic north and maintain the correct direction of travel.

Tracking & Recording Equipment
Control Card (SI-Card / Electronic Chip)
A device used to prove you visited the checkpoints. It can be a traditional paper card for manual punching, or a digital finger chip that logs times electronically.
Control Description Sheet
A small slip containing international symbols that explain the exact location of each checkpoint (e.g., "at the eastern base of the boulder").

Apparel & Personal Gear
Orienteering Suit
Lightweight, breathable, and durable nylon or synthetic clothing (usually long-sleeved) designed to protect the skin from scratches while running through dense woods.
Gaiters / Shin Guards
Protective sleeves worn over the lower legs to shield the shins from briars, thorns, and sharp undergrowth.
Orienteering Shoes (Studded Shoes)
Specialized running shoes with aggressive rubber or metal studs on the soles to provide maximum grip on muddy, wet, or steep terrain.

Safety & Accessory Equipment
Whistle
A mandatory safety device used strictly to signal for emergency assistance in case of severe injury or getting hopelessly lost.
Map Magnifier
A magnifying glass that clips onto the thumb compass or hand, helping the runner read tiny map details while on the move.
Control Description Holder
A transparent plastic sleeve strapped to the forearm for quick, hands-free reading of the checkpoint descriptions.
Safety Glasses
Clear, lightweight glasses worn to protect the eyes from low-hanging branches and twigs when bursting through thick vegetation.
